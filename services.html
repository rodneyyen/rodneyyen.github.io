Many sports-related injuries are regularly seen at our clinic. Heel pain such as Plantar Fasciitis is a common yet debilitating injury that can either involve one or both feet. Ankle pain and instablity is frequently found in active individuals. Tendonitis, arthritis, inflammed ligaments, and pulled muscles can all cause unnecessary discomfort. Bony spurs continue to grow if not addressed. Fractures, or broken bones, and sprains may necessitate a period of non-weight bearing and immobilization. Tendon injuries, such as pulls and Achilles Tendon Ruptures present with various treatment options. Many of the above situations require the urgent evaluation and treatment to obtain full recovery.

In addition to sports-related injuries, many more common complaints are regularly addressed. Ingrown toenails can be quite painful. Infections, including fungus of the nails and other diseased states present in all age group. People with plantar's warts can have them painlessly removed. Patients with diabetes are advised by the American Diabetic Association to have their feet checked annually. Skin conditions, such as rashes, burns, and athletes feet are all successfully attended to. Bunions are projections of the big toe bone and be difficult to fit into regular shoes. Corns, callouses, and cracks in the skin can be easily rectified with various protocols. Arthritis including gout can all be relieved. All these issues are addressed with various options to help you get back on your feet with minimal "down time".

When the need arises, Dr. Yen advise you on not only conservative options but also address your case from the surgical aspect. With staff privileges at various local hospitals, the necessity to maintain his continuing medical education with the latest techniques throughout his career is addressed. He has written numerous articles for the "Journal of the American Podiatric Medical Association" and has served in various capacities for the Washington State Podiatric Medical Association. When the necessity for surgery presents itself, you cannot find yourself in the hands of better care.
